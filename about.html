      About me

Hey, I’m Ben.

 

I am a young, budding Web Designer/Developer and avid participator in the Game Changer project, funded by the Real Ideas Organisation. The project focuses on engaging with Young Adults who are not in education, employment or training, with activities that can help them gain a route into one of these down the line.

My Backstory

I attended Redruth School for five years, and after my GCSE’s, stayed on at the Redruth School Sixth Form for a year before leaving in November 2017 because iit didn't suit what I wanted to do for the future. I've been looking since then, for a route into the Technology sector.

 

Being only 17 years old, I joined in the Game Changer project, and have participated in loads of events since then, such as a trip up to the Digi Publish event in London, part of the World’s forum Disrupt group, as well as creating Chat Bots with the Google Development group Falmouth and one of the Mission to Mars events, where for a week, we used agile techniques to make robots performed user stories that we had to programme.

 

Since I left Sixth Form, I have been looking to grow my own skills, both in terms of social and programming skills to now know how to create  my own website, programme some robots from the ‘Mission to Mars’  event using Audino and creating a chat-bot via Google’s Dialogue flow.

What’s next?

I want to within the next few years, end up in a situation where I can become an Apprentice Software Developer, or an intern Software developer within a company and gather experience in a place of work, whilst working on projects and ideas that pop into my head. Hopefully this can help me become more confident in promoting myself and making myself some opportunities.

Interests

My interests are quite easy to nail down, I think. I love technology that can make people’s lives easier to live, I like tabletop games, and have done since I was a little boy, having played Warhammer 40,000 as a child alongside Magic the Gathering and Yu-Gi-Oh, the collectible trading card games. I’ve recently been fascinated with Dungeons and Dragons, having played it and not been able to get enough of it. Other things include newer forms of entertainment in the likes of YouTube and Twitch, but also some of Britain’s old classic comedy normally featured on the TV network Gold, such as Only Fools and Horses, the Two Ronnies, ectera.
